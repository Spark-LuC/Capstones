### 1. Title 
Performance Concentration and Collective Effort in Virtual Teams: Evidence from 
Competitive Play in Valorant. 
### 2. Abstract 
This research aims to discuss in high-pressure virtual environments how the unequal 
performance distribution within the teams affects the collective effort and outcomes. Based 
on social loafing and moral hazard theories, examining whether extreme concentration of 
individual performance, commonly referred to by players as ‘carrying’, is systematically 
associated with the average contributions of other teammates. This research will focus on 
the longitudinal panel data from the competitive Valorant matches, and analyse how 
performance concentration interacts with resource reallocation and role differentiation. 
Finally, identifying conditions under which individual excellence can be beneficial or harmful 
to collective efforts. 
### 3. Description of Data Collection 
This research will use the public data from the competitive Valorant matches, which includes 
match metadata, individual performance indicators, and team-level measures of 
performance concentration, in-game resource allocation(game credits and weapon transfer), 
rank and role differentiation, and relevant controlled variables like fixed map and patch. 
#### 3.1 Data Collection 
Data will be collected by Python that accesses the HenrikDev unofficial Valorant API to get 
public match history from high-skill tiers, and uses the stats on vlr.gg to analyse the 
professional players as supplementary data. 
#### 3.2 Potential Collection Issues 
Potential issues include selection bias(data mainly collected from the high-performing 
players) and behavioural heterogeneity across game patches. All the player identifiers(like 
names) will be anonymised in compliance with Riot Games’ developer policies
